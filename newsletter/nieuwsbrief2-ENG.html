Hi fellow alumni,


This is already the second newsletter of Ada Alumni! Over the past few months, we have organized our first activity; a social drink, which was in our opinion a big success! We were able to welcome both recent and long-time graduates, who had a drink together and could catch up!

That having said, we are looking forward to future activities, of which the first one is right around the corner! In this newsletter, you can find more about our next activity! Additionally, the department is looking for graduates from Computer Science who like to speak to current students, and for graduates of Embedded Systems because of the redesign of the current curriculum of the master program.

Do you have something interesting to share via the newsletter? Do you have a good idea for the next activity or do you want to organize something yourself? Let us know!

Kind regards,

On behalf Ada Alumni

Activity at TU/e X-mas market


Christmas is just around the corner, and to get in festive spirits the university is organizing a Christmas Market in the Atlas building. The event will take place on weekdays between December 7th till December 21st starting at 16:00h and ending at 20:00h. During the market there will be stands where Christmas shopping can be done, as well as where you can enjoy a bite to eat and beverage to drink.

During the evening of the 13th of December (Tuesday), a special event for Ada Alumni members will be organized. The evening will start at 17:30h, and besides time to stroll around the market we will also organize a special speech from a special guest (at 18:00h), updating you about all the recent events and updates regarding the research in our field. After this, there will be a special spot at the market where you can join us for some catching up, having a drink and getting into the Christmas spirit.

To have a nice memory of visiting the TU/e Christmas Market with alumni, Alumni Relations will hand out a unique collector's item as a present, because what is Christmas without a little present?

Please subscribe here so we can estimate how many people will be attending.

We hope to see you there!

Alumni Night Computer Science


Dear alumni,

This December the Department of M&CS organizes the Alumni Night for the bachelor computer science students again. During this event, 2nd/3rd year bachelor students will meet in small groups with alumni from our computer science programs to discuss their career and current job. This way, students become aware of the broad spectrum of companies and jobs they can opt for, after which they might update their choice of master's and bachelor electives if necessary. In previous years, the event was highly appreciated by students and alumni.

Did you graduate from any of the bachelor or master programs in computer science at TU/e and do you want to join the event on the evening of December 6, 2022? Please fill out this form. For alumni we will arrange dinner before the start of the event and there will be a reception at Hubble after the event. More practical information such as the program of the evening can also be found on the first page of this form.

Wietske & Rink
alumninight.mcs@tue.nl
Projectteam Alumni Night 2022, Department of Mathematics and Computer Science

Program Management is looking for alumni of Embedded Systems


Program Management of Computer Science & Embedded Systems has asked us to share the following message, specifically intended for Embedded Systems alumni.

As a Master graduate in Embedded Systems, we believe you hold a valuable degree. In order to keep our ES program valuable also for future students we assess our program every couple of years. This year that assessment is even more important as it coincides with the re-accreditation of our Program by the NVAO. So, we could really use your help in checking if our Program still provides the skills and knowledge needed for future ES engineers. Would you help us gaining insights by filling out this survey? This should not take you more than 10 minutes.

Thanks in advance,
On behalf of Program Management Embedded Systems,

Katinka Verschuren
Program Coordinator Computer Science & Embedded Systems
