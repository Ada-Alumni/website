Hi fellow alumni,


This is already the third newsletter of Ada Alumni! Over the past few months, we have organized our second activity at the TU/e
X-mas market. Many alumni joined us, listening to the talk of our dean, Edwin van den Heuvel, and having a drink together.

That having said, we are looking forward to future activities, of which the first one is right around the corner! In this newsletter, you can find more about our next activity!

Do you have something interesting to share via the newsletter? Do you have a good idea for the next activity or do you want to organize something yourself? Let us know!

Kind regards,

On behalf of Ada Alumni

Exhibition ‘25 illustrious women in Computer Science’


Between March 3rd and March 24th, there is an exhibition in MetaForum of 25 illustrious women in Computer Science, featuring also a number of TU/e employees, such as Anna Vilanova and Bettina Speckmann. The exhibition takes place in MetaForum at the right balustrade in front of the library.

TU/e Celebrations for International Women’s Day (March 8th)


This year’s theme for International Women’s Day is “Pushing the limits for a livable world.” How can we dream a future into being without the limitations of climate change, ecological breakdown, pandemics, scarcity, and other threats to a peaceful living environment? How can we create a world where livability is limitless?

Join for an inspiring evening, with talks and interviews with female TU/e students, staff and alumna who are working towards a better world.

For more infomation and the exact program, you can have a look at this page. Book your (free) seat here.

GEWIS board announcement


Dear alumni,

The board of GEWIS changed their internal activity regulations which might affect you when subscribing to one of our activities. From now on, inactive graduated members (members who are not studying anymore and are not part of a committee or fraternity) will be placed on the waiting list automatically if the maximum capacity of participants is reached. There might be some exceptions which will then be stated in the activity description.

If something is unclear, feel free to come to the GEWIS room and ask the board or e-mail intern@gewis.nl.
